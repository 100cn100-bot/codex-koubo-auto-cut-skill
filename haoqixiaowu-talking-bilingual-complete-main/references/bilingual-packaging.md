# Bilingual Packaging

## Goal

Create packaging that still feels native in Chinese layout, while also being understandable to English-speaking viewers.

This is not literal translation.

This is bilingual communication design.

## Priority

1. Preserve the Chinese visual hierarchy.
2. Preserve scene meaning.
3. Help English-speaking viewers understand quickly.
4. Avoid turning the layout into two competing headline systems.

## Default Pattern

Use:

- Chinese as the primary headline or main label
- English as secondary support text

Typical pairing:

- Chinese title
- English subtitle

or:

- Chinese KPI label
- English caption

or:

- Chinese process step
- English support label

## English Writing Rules

- Write concise English.
- Prefer meaning over literal wording.
- Avoid overly formal translation.
- Use short sentence fragments when the Chinese line is also fragmentary.
- Translate for comprehension, not for lexical completeness.

Examples:

- `不能无中生有`
  Better: `Do not fabricate details`
  Worse: `You cannot create something from nothing`

- `更高级的 Photoshop`
  Better: `A more advanced Photoshop`
  Worse: `A higher-level PS software tool`

- `把工作流拆开`
  Better: `Break the workflow into stages`
  Worse: `Disassemble the workflow process`

## Layout Rules

- English should usually be 35% to 55% of the Chinese display size.
- English should be visually secondary.
- English should often use a lighter color or secondary emphasis.
- If space is tight, shorten English first.
- Do not let bilingual text cause overflow.

## Scene-Level Rules

### Hook scenes

- Chinese carries the emotional punch
- English explains the idea clearly

### Data scenes

- Chinese for the main metric label
- English for concise metric meaning

### Concept scenes

- Chinese for the concept name
- English for its explanatory gloss

### Workflow scenes

- Chinese for step labels
- English for short operational meaning

### CTA scenes

- Chinese can stay conversational
- English should be short and action-oriented

## Final Check

Before finalizing bilingual packaging, verify:

- an English-speaking viewer can understand the scene without hearing the original Chinese
- the English text does not overpower the Chinese composition
- the layout still reads cleanly from a distance
